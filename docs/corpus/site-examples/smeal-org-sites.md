# Smeal Student Organization Website Analysis

> Last updated: 2026-03-24
> Category: site-examples
> Confidence: Community (direct site analysis)

---

## Key Finding: The Bar Is Low

Most Smeal orgs either have no standalone website (OrgCentral/Discover only) or use free-tier Weebly/Wix with dated templates. The "best" sites are Squarespace. Nobody is running a custom-built React/Next.js site. A well-designed site immediately sets Applied AI apart from 90%+ of Smeal organizations.

## Tier 1: Best Smeal Org Sites

### Phi Beta Lambda -- https://www.psupbl.com/ (Squarespace, 8/10)
- Clean, modern with full-width hero images, professional photography
- Four-pillar framework (professionalism, philanthropy, social, competition)
- Employer logos showing where alumni work -- credibility play
- "Big Business, Bigger Family" tagline
- Penn State branding: subtle, "Pennsylvania State University's chapter" in subtitle

### Alpha Kappa Psi -- https://www.akpsipsu.com/ (Squarespace, 7.5/10)
- Dark navy/professional color scheme, white space
- Clear recruitment pathway, president's welcome message
- "Shaping People. Shaping Business" value prop
- Penn State branding: minimal, identifies as chapter name

### Happy Valley VC -- https://happyvalley.vc/ (Static HTML, 7.5/10)
- Minimalist, monochromatic, clean
- Direct CTAs: "Join our team, Pitch Comp, Book a coffee chat"
- The .vc domain is a branding win

### Nittany Lion Fund -- https://nittanylionfund.psu.edu/ (WordPress, 7/10)
- "Penn State Students Managing Millions" -- incredible tagline
- Hosted on .psu.edu domain, institutional weight
- Penn State branding: strong (PSU_BUS_RGB_2C logo, official domain)

## Broader Penn State High Bar Sites

### THON -- https://thon.org/ (WordPress, 9/10)
- Gold standard. Navy + bright yellow. Hero slider, countdown timer.
- Clear CTAs, fundraising UX, strong visual hierarchy.

### HackPSU -- https://hackpsu.org/ (Next.js + React, 8/10)
- Most technically sophisticated student org site at Penn State
- Custom-built, modern stack, open source on GitHub
- The tech itself demonstrates capabilities

### Nittany AI Alliance -- https://nittanyai.psu.edu/ (Craft CMS, 8.5/10)
- Closest comparable to our site. "AI for Good, Powered by Penn State"
- Three pathways (Students, Faculty, Partners)
- University-backed, explains the production quality

### Lion Ambassadors -- https://www.lionambassadors.com/ (Squarespace, 8/10)
- Strong institutional identity, clear recruitment funnel
- Four hero themes, newsletter signup

## Business School AI Clubs at Other Schools

### Chicago Booth AI Club -- https://groups.chicagobooth.edu/aiclub/home/ (8/10)
- Three membership tiers, three personas ("Explorer/Strategist/Builder")
- Real metrics: 8 officers, 62 members, 28 events
- Cross-industry AI focus, inclusive of all experience levels

### MIT Sloan AI & ML Club -- https://sloangroups.mit.edu/AIML/ (7.5/10)
- Three pillars: "Learn, Network, Build"
- Speakers from OpenAI, Microsoft, Google, Meta, AWS, Waymo

### Stanford AI Club -- https://aiclub.stanford.edu/ (Next.js, 7/10)
- "Stanford's home for students in AI" -- simple, inclusive positioning
- Modern tech stack, dark theme

### Harvard Business School AI Club (4/10)
- Standard HBS page template. Just text with contact info.
- Even at HBS, the AI club has a terrible web presence. Name brand carries the load.

### WUDAC (Wharton) -- https://wudac.org/ (Squarespace, 8/10)
- Concrete metrics, partner credibility, specific skills listed

## Patterns Worth Borrowing

1. **Hero with clear tagline** -- "Penn State Students Managing Millions" (NLF), "AI for Good, Powered by Penn State" (Nittany AI)
2. **Three pillars/personas** -- Chicago Booth's "Explorer/Strategist/Builder", PBL's 4 pillars
3. **Employer/partner logos** -- cheap credibility
4. **Clear join CTA** -- every good site makes recruitment the primary conversion
5. **Member spotlights** -- PBL and Lion Ambassadors use these effectively
6. **Events/calendar** -- shows the org is active
7. **Real metrics** -- "62 members, 28 events" builds trust
